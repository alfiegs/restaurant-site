But one cool, collected bite into this banh uot thit nuong, as the rolls are called in Vietnamese, and I allowed an unaccustomed thought to form: Maybe Huynh was the Houston Vietnamese restaurant I had been waiting for all these years. A few more bites and I was sure of it. The delicate satin of the noodle wrapper, the subtle crustiness of the savorysweet grilled pork, the crunch and spring of frilled leaf lettuce, the zing of fresh mint and cilantro all conspired to astonish. “This is one of the best things I’ve eaten in ages,” I murmured to my lunch companion. “It’s perfect.” Continue Reading
